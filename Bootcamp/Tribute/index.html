Steve Jobs

Stay Hungry Stay Foolish

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs Life:

  • 1955 - Was born in San Francisco, the son of Abdulfattah Jandali and Joanne Schieble. He is quickly adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs
  • 1960 - The Jobs family moves from San Francisco to Mountain View, a suburban town in Santa Clara county, more famous under the name Silicon Valley.
  • 1968 - 13-year-old Steve Jobs calls up Bill Hewlett and gets a summer job at the HP factory.
  • 1969 - Steve Jobs meets Steve Wozniak, 5 years older, through a mutual friend. Woz and Steve share a love of electronics.
  • 1972 - Steve and Woz build and sell 'blue boxes' that allow to make phone calls for free.
  • 1973 - Steve spends a semester at Reed College, Oregon, then drops out. He will stay on campus and attend the classes that interest him for a while.
  • 1974 - Steve gets his first job at video game maker Atari, and later makes a trip to India to 'seek enlightenment'.
  • 1976 - Apple Computer Inc. is incorporated by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ron Wayne. Steve and Woz start assembling Apple I computers and sell them to computer hobbyists.
  • 1978 - The Apple II becomes the first mass-market personal computer, with impressive sales around the US. Apple becomes a symbol of the personal computing revolution
  • 1979 - Steve Jobs is shown the first working graphical user interface at Xerox PARC.
  • 1983 - PepsiCo CEO John Sculley becomes Apple's CEO after having been wooed by Steve Jobs for several months
  • 1985 - Apple's board sides with John Sculley and strips Steve off all executive duties. Steve Jobs resigns from Apple and starts NeXT.
  • 1986 - Jobs buys the computer division of George Lucas film for $10 million and incorporates it as Pixar
  • 1991 - Steve Jobs marries Laurene Powell in Yosemite under the blessing of Steve's old zen guru Kobin Chino.
  • 1995 - Steve starts focusing less on NeXT and more on Pixar before Toy Story is released. One week after Toy Story is out, Pixar goes public. Steve Jobs's worth rises to $1.5 billion.
  • 1996 - Apple, which was desperately looking for a modern operating system to buy, eventually buys NeXT for $400 million. Steve Jobs is named "informal adviser" to Apple CEO Gil Amelio.
  • 1997 - Apple starts its 'Think Different' campaign to restore its damaged brand image.
  • 1998 - At Macworld San Francisco, Steve Jobs announces that Apple is profitable again, thanks to sales of the new Power Macintosh computers
  • 2001 - After an 8-month crash development program, Steve Jobs unveils iPod at a small media event on the company's campus.
  • 2004 - Steve unveils the iPod mini. The iPod mini will soon become the world's best-selling MP3 player and truly establish Apple as a consumer electronics powerhouse
  • 2005 - Steve Jobs makes a memorable commencement speech at Stanford University. History will remember its closing remarks, Steve's advice to the young students: 'Stay hungry, stay foolish'
  • 2007 - In his most memorable keynote presentation ever, at Macworld 2007, Steve Jobs introduces iPhone and its revolutionary touch-screen interface.
  • 2008 - Apple announces it will open the iPhone platform to outside developers with the App Store.
  • 2010 - After months of wild rumors, Steve Jobs unveils iPad, 'the biggest thing Apple's ever done'. The tablet runs the same operating system as iPhone
  • 2011 - Steve Jobs resigns as CEO of Apple. Tim Cook becomes Apple CEO. Steve Jobs dies at home, surrounded by his family
